# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions
- Works with QA Lead on test coverage and acceptance criteria validation
- Collaborates with UX Designer on implementation feasibility
- Partners with Release Manager on deployment readiness

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions
- Works with Business Analyst to refine requirements
- Collaborates with UX Designer on user research and design validation
- Partners with Stakeholders on priority alignment

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions
- Coordinates with Release Manager on deployment timelines
- Works with QA Lead on testing schedules and quality gates
- Partners with all team members for status tracking and escalation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the testing strategy and quality assurance activities for projects. They ensure that acceptance criteria are met, quality gates are passed, and issues are identified before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategy and test plans for features
- Coordinate QA activities and test execution
- Establish and enforce quality gates and acceptance criteria
- Triage and document defects with clear reproduction steps
- Collaborate on automation frameworks and CI integration
- Ensure test coverage aligns with risk and priority
- Provide quality metrics and testing status reports

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Enable fast, confident releases
- Improve test efficiency through automation
- Maintain high quality standards while enabling velocity

### Typical Communication
- Daily QA standups and status updates
- Test plan and defect documentation
- Quality metrics and gate status reports
- Collaboration with developers on bug fixes and clarifications

### Interactions
- Works closely with Developers on test coverage and bug reproduction
- Partners with Product Manager to understand and validate acceptance criteria
- Collaborates with UX Designer on user experience testing
- Coordinates with Release Manager on pre-release quality verification
- Reports to Project Manager on quality risks and blockers

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for researching, designing, and validating user experiences. They ensure that features are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and gather requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Validate design concepts with users and stakeholders
- Provide design guidance and feedback during development
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met
- Collaborate on user acceptance testing and feedback
- Document design decisions and design systems

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, intuitive experiences
- Reduce support burden through thoughtful design
- Maintain consistency and accessibility across products
- Enable data-driven design decisions

### Typical Communication
- Design specs and prototypes
- User research findings and insights
- Design review comments and feedback
- Usability testing reports and recommendations

### Interactions
- Collaborates with Product Manager on user research and validation
- Works with Developers on implementation feasibility and design handoff
- Partners with QA Lead on usability and acceptance testing
- Gathers feedback from Support Engineer on user pain points

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and the technical team. They gather requirements, clarify business rules, and ensure that solutions align with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Write clear user stories and acceptance criteria
- Clarify business rules and edge cases
- Conduct impact analysis on proposed changes
- Participate in design reviews to validate alignment with business needs
- Support testing and validation with business context
- Create and maintain requirements documentation

### Goals
- Ensure solutions align with business objectives
- Reduce rework due to misunderstood requirements
- Enable effective communication between business and technical teams
- Improve requirements clarity and completeness

### Typical Communication
- User stories and acceptance criteria documentation
- Requirements gathering and clarification sessions
- Impact analysis reports
- Business rule documentation

### Interactions
- Works closely with Stakeholders to gather requirements
- Partners with Product Manager on backlog refinement
- Collaborates with Developers to clarify requirements during implementation
- Supports QA Lead in understanding business acceptance criteria

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee the release process, ensuring that features are properly tested, documented, and deployed with minimal risk. They coordinate the go/no-go decision and manage rollback scenarios.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release plans and schedules
- Coordinate release activities across teams
- Ensure release documentation is complete and accurate
- Establish go/no-go criteria and gate approvals
- Document rollback and incident response procedures
- Communicate release status and timing to stakeholders
- Manage release notes and deployment instructions
- Conduct post-release verification and monitoring

### Goals
- Enable safe, predictable releases
- Minimize deployment risk and downtime
- Ensure clear communication and coordination
- Support rapid incident response if needed

### Typical Communication
- Release plans and schedules
- Release notes and deployment instructions
- Go/no-go gate assessments
- Incident and rollback communications

### Interactions
- Coordinates with Project Manager on overall timeline and priorities
- Works with Developers and QA Lead on release readiness
- Partners with Support Engineer on post-release monitoring and support
- Reports to Stakeholders on release status and timing

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders represent business, customer, or organizational interests. They provide inputs, requirements, approvals, and feedback on deliverables to ensure alignment with broader business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business context, goals, and constraints
- Review and approve key deliverables and milestones
- Provide feedback on prototypes and solutions
- Participate in decision gates (go/no-go decisions)
- Support user acceptance testing and validation
- Communicate project updates to their teams or departments
- Escalate blockers and dependencies to leadership

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers business value
- Maintain alignment across organizational priorities
- Enable informed decision-making on scope and trade-offs
- Drive adoption and support for delivered solutions

### Typical Communication
- Status updates and milestone reviews
- Approval and feedback on key decisions
- Participation in demos and reviews
- Business case and success metrics

### Interactions
- Partners with Product Manager on strategic alignment and priorities
- Works with Business Analyst to define business requirements
- Reviews deliverables with UX Designer and Development team
- Coordinates with Release Manager on go-live readiness
- Escalates issues and decisions via Project Manager

---

## Support Engineer

### Role Summary
Support Engineers manage post-release support, field inquiries, and gather feedback from customers and operations. They are the voice of the customer and provide valuable insights for continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Manage customer support and incidents post-release
- Triage and document support tickets with reproduction steps
- Communicate issues to the development team
- Provide feedback on product usability and pain points
- Support customer training and documentation
- Monitor system health and performance post-release
- Escalate critical issues and participate in incident response
- Gather usage and feedback data for product improvements

### Goals
- Ensure customer satisfaction and success
- Reduce support burden through better product design
- Identify and escalate critical issues quickly
- Provide actionable feedback for product improvements

### Typical Communication
- Support ticket documentation and summaries
- Incident reports and escalations
- Customer feedback and trends analysis
- Post-release monitoring reports

### Interactions
- Escalates issues to Developers and QA Lead for investigation
- Works with Release Manager on hotfix coordination and communication
- Provides feedback to UX Designer on user experience issues
- Reports to Product Manager on user patterns and pain points
- Coordinates with Project Manager on support resource needs
